Microsatellites are tandem repeats of DNA with repeat lengths of 6 base pairs or less. They are also known as simple sequence repeats (SSRs). These have been reported in the genomes of plants and animals including that of humans. Some of the most common microsatellite sequences are (AT)n, (AG)n, (AAT)n and (ATTT)n. SSRs may be present close to genes or even within genes. Those that very close to genes can be used as markers linked to the genes. SSRs are that are present within genes are known as genic SSR or genic microsatellites. These can be used for identifying the genes and/or analyzing the defects in the genes.

Mining for microsatellites in the DNA databases is an inexpensive approach of discovering these important sequences.

Mining for SSRs in the gene sequence databases of tomato will help to locate the presence of genes, such as disease resistance in the genome of cultivated tomato and its wild relatives. Such study will lead to isolation of important genes and also assist in studying the molecular nature of genes. Information on molecular nature of genes is essential to gain more understanding on the function of genes.
